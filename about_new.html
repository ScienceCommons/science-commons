Science-Commons

Know the Scientific Record.


Science-Commons' mission is to accelerate the growth of cumulative knowledge and innovation by organizing and curating key information about scientific articles. This includes available raw data and analysis syntax code, experimental materials, post-publication peer review comments and blog posts, and consolidated evidence of follow-up independent replications.


Much of the information will be automatically aggregated via APIs and data mining from pre-existing sources. For example, raw data/syntax and materials will be aggregated from Figshare.com, Datadryad.org, and the OpenScienceFramework.org. Post-publication comments will be consolidated from PubMedCommons, PubPeer.com, and PLoS and Frontiers journals. Information regarding follow-up independent replications (and other information) will be incorporated via crowd-sourcing or a combination of automated aggregation/data mining and crowd-sourced verification.


Organizing such information for each scientific article has numerous benefits including facilitating the critique, evaluation, extension, application, analysis, re-analysis, reproducibility, and interpretation of research findings. Organizing this information will also facilitate the execution of independent replications and yield new theoretical insights via more sophisticated forms of real-time and publication-bias-corrected meta-analyses.
